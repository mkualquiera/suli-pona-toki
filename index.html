Overview

suli pona toki is a dialect of toki pona inspired by Japanese sentence structure and Forth-like stack-based processing. It introduces several significant structural changes while maintaining toki pona's vocabulary and philosophical simplicity.

Key Innovations

  • Particle-Based Grammar: All grammatical functions are expressed through suffix particles
  • Head-Final Structure: Modifiers precede the words they modify
  • The "no" Connector: Creates possesive or attribute-holding grouping
  • The "an-" Prefix: Systematically creates opposites
  • The "lu-" Particle: Provides a pragmatic layer for expressing the speaker's perspective
  • Subject-Object-Verb structure: Allows more easily talking about multiple objects in a single sentence

Grammatical Particles

Particles in suli pona toki are attached as suffixes to their associated content words:

Suffix Function Original Word Example
-su Subject marker suli (important) jansu = "person (as subject)"
-pa Verb/action marker pali (do/work) tawapa = "go (as action)"
-jo Object marker jo (have) tomojo = "house (as object)"
-ta To/toward tawa (to/toward) tomota = "to the house"
-na From/because tan (from) tomona = "from the house"
-ki Using/with kepeken (use) iloki = "with a tool"
-lo At/in/on lon (at) tomolo = "at the house"
-la Context marker la (if/when) monsi tenpola = "before..."
-en And en (and) mije janen meli jan = "the man and the woman"
-nu Or/alternative anu (or) minu sina = "you or I"
-lu Meta-commentary lukin (to see) taso lonlu = "but of course...",

Example Sentences

ponajan su lape ki tomo ta tawa pa
The good person walks sleepily to the house.
tonsijan su sinatomo jo milon lu pona pa
The non-binary person, honestly, improves your house.
telotomo lo jan su leko jo tawa pa
The person at the wet house moves the box.

Modifier Structure

suli pona toki uses a head-final tailhead structure where modifiers (tail) precede the words they modify (head), and provides advanced ways to group concepts.

Simple Modification

Modifiers precede the words they modify:

nasatelo
crazy water = alcohol
palisasitelenilo
stick-like drawing tool = a pen
sinasulimunlukalinja
yours great celestial arm line = (the big celestial line-shaped arm) of yours

Modification with "pi"

The "pi" particle creates a new semantic unit, where what follows it becomes the "core" concept, and what precedes it becomes a modifier:

sina pona jan pi sitelen ilo
yours good person -> writing tool = the pen that your friend owns
sina pona jan pi sitelen ilo pi lukin pona
(yours good person -> writing tool) -> looking good = the good looking quality of your friend's pen

Advanced Modification with "no"

The "no" particle can be thought of as turning the current deepest head into an explanation, property, or tail for what is to come. Consider the following pi-examples:

nasa telo pi ike jan
crazy water -> bad person = The villain who is known for brewing alcohol
nasa telo pi ike jan pi suli telo
(crazy water -> bad person) -> big house= The mansion owned by the villain who is known for brewing alcohol

In the first example, the semantic head is "ike jan", and the tail is "nasa telo". This means that we are specifically talking about a bad person. However, what if we were not talking about said bad person, but instead about something they own or some property of theirs? This is where the "no" particle comes in:

nasa telo pi ike jan no suli tomo
crazy water -> (bad person -> big house) = The villain who is known for brewing alcohol

The "an-" Prefix System

The "an-" prefix can be attached to any content word to express its conceptual opposite. Unlike simple negation with "ala", the "an-" prefix creates a true opposite concept.

Original With "an-" Standard toki pona Equivalent
pona (good) an-pona ike (bad)
suli (big) an-suli lili (small)
moli (death) an-moli lon (existence)
open (open) an-open pini (close/end)
wawa (strong) an-wawa wawa ala (not strong)

Example Usage

anpona jansu ansuli tomojo pakalapa
The bad person destroys the small house.
mi lonlu ansona jansu wawa kepekipa anwawa iloki
Honestly, the ignorant person strongly uses a weak tool.

The "-lu" Perspective Marker

The "-lu" suffix transforms phrases into discourse markers and adverbials that express the speaker's perspective or attitude toward what's being said. It creates meta-commentary without requiring additional vocabulary.

Types of "-lu" Expressions

Discourse connectors

  • taso lonlu = "but of course..."
  • ante nasinlu = "anyway..."
  • taso kenlu = "but also..."

Speaker attitude markers

  • mi lonlu = "honestly" (lit: "from my truth")
  • suli ponalu = "Amazingly" (lit: "from a greatly good perspective")
  • kin nasinlu = "by the way" (lit: "also from this path perspective")

Example Sentences

mi lonlu ni musisu anponapa
Honestly, this game is not good.
taso kenlu misu awen tawapa tomota
But also, I will continue going to the house.
suli ponalu ni tenposu ponapa mute janta
Amazingly, many people feel good about this time. (lit. 'this time is good for many people')
